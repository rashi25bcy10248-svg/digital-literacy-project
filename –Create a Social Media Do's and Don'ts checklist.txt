 task 4 Create a Social Media Do's and Don'ts checklist




Do’s


* The Three-Second Rule: Just pause before hitting "post." Seriously. Ask yourself, "Would I be okay with this being on a billboard in five years?" If the answer is even a "maybe not," just delete the draft. It’s not worth the future headache.
* Audit Your Privacy Settings: I know, it’s the most boring "to-do" list item ever. But platforms change their terms all the time. It actually matters who can see your high school photos or your late-night rants.
* Fact-Check Before You Forward: It’s so easy to just hit "share" on something that looks shocking. But spreading fake info happens way more than we think. If it sounds too wild to be true, it probably is.
* Keep a "Professional-ish" Vibe: You don't have to be a suit-and-tie version of yourself, but remember that professors and recruiters will Google you. You want them to see someone they’d actually want to work with, not just your 2 a.m. meme collection.
* Watch Your Tone in the Comments: Sarcasm is basically impossible to read online. A joke that sounds hilarious in your head can come across as aggressive or just plain mean to someone else.

 The "Don’ts" 
* Don't Treat the Internet Like Your Diary: Putting your phone number, home address, or ID details out there is just asking for trouble. It’s really not worth the risk, no matter how much you "trust" your followers.
* Avoid the "Troll" Bait: It’s so tempting to get into a comment war with a stranger, but it’s a total trap. Those fights get serious really fast and they never actually end well for anyone involved.
* Stop Blindly Accepting Requests: I get it, we all want a high follower count. But not everyone online is who they say they are. If you don't know them in real life (or have a very good reason to), just hit "ignore."
* Don't Overshare Your Location: Posting exactly where you are or your daily routine (like "I'm at this gym every Tuesday at 5!") can actually be pretty unsafe. Keep the "live" updates for when you've already left the building.
* Ignore the "Too Good to be True" Links: Those "You won a free iPhone!" or "Check out this leaked video!" links are almost always scams. Even if they look legit or come from a friend’s (probably hacked) account, just don't click.
